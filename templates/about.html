Core Game Rules

Game Board Structure

The game is played on a 9×9 grid divided into nine 3×3 sub-grids. Each sub-grid functions as an independent tic-tac-toe board with standard win conditions.

Victory Condition

Win three sub-grids in a row (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) to claim overall victory. Each sub-grid win counts as one position in the larger game.

Movement Constraint

Your move determines which sub-grid your opponent must play on next. This creates the strategic depth that defines the game.

Strategic Mechanics

Sending Rule

The position of your move within a sub-grid determines which sub-grid your opponent must play on next. This creates a chain of strategic decisions.

Completed Boards

When sent to a completed or full sub-grid, you gain the freedom to play anywhere on the board. Use this strategically.

Multi-Level Thinking

Success requires thinking on multiple levels: immediate sub-grid tactics and long-term board positioning strategy.

Advanced Tactics

Opening Control

Prioritize center sub-grids early. Center positions provide maximum flexibility and control over opponent movement.

Threat Creation

Build multiple simultaneous threats across different sub-grids. Force opponents into impossible defensive positions.

Board Manipulation

Send opponents to disadvantageous sub-grids. Avoid giving them access to empty boards where they can establish control.

Strategic Sacrifice

Sometimes conceding a sub-grid maintains overall control. Don't let opponents dictate the game flow through forced wins.

Forcing Sequences

Create chains of forced moves that lead to favorable positions. Advanced players can plan 3-4 moves ahead.

Endgame Precision

In endgame scenarios, calculate all possible outcomes. Sometimes drawing a sub-grid prevents opponent victories.
