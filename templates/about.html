// Strategic Constraint

‚ö° The Sending Rule

Your move determines which local board your opponent must play on next. If you play in the top-right cell of a local board, your opponent must play somewhere in the top-right local board.

üö´ Completed Boards

If you're sent to a local board that's already won or completely filled, you can play anywhere on the entire board.

üéØ Strategic Depth

This constraint creates incredible strategic depth. You must think not only about winning the current local board, but also about where you'll send your opponent and what opportunities that creates.

// Advanced Strategy

Opening Strategy

Control the center local board early. Center moves give you the most flexibility and put pressure on your opponent.

Board Control

Try to win local boards in strategic positions. Corner boards are powerful, but edge boards can create surprising threats.

Sending Tactics

Send your opponent to boards where they have limited good options. Avoid sending them to empty boards where they can set up threats.

Defensive Play

Sometimes it's better to sacrifice a local board to maintain control. Don't let your opponent dictate the flow of the game.

Forcing Moves

Create threats that force your opponent into bad positions. Multiple simultaneous threats are often game-winning.

Endgame

In the endgame, count your threats carefully. Sometimes drawing a local board is better than losing it.
